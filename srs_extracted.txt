1. Introduction

1.1 Purpose

This Systems Requirements Specification (SRS) defines the complete functional and non-functional requirements for a Multi-Store E-Commerce and Price Comparison Platform. It is the authoritative reference document for the development team, business analysts, quality assurance engineers, and all project stakeholders throughout the system lifecycle.

Version 2.0 integrates competitive benchmarking findings drawn from five leading platforms — Jumia Kenya, PriceCheck South Africa, Google Shopping, Price.com, and Shopify — to ensure the system is commercially viable, feature-competitive, and aligned with proven industry patterns.



1.2 Scope

The platform is a web-based multi-vendor marketplace enabling sellers to self-onboard and manage product catalogues, and buyers to discover, compare prices, and purchase from multiple stores in a single session. The system scope encompasses:

Seller onboarding, identity verification, and catalogue management (individual and bulk)

Buyer registration, real-time multi-store search, price comparison, cart, and checkout

AI-assisted price intelligence and lowest-price highlighting across all registered stores

Community social layer: product discussions, ratings, reviews, and follow features

Company profile, supplier directory, and how-to-sell static content pages

Platform administration: user management, content moderation, analytics, and reporting

Commission management, seller performance tracking, and payout dashboards

2. System Overview

2.1 Business Context & Market Opportunity

The platform targets a demonstrable market gap in the ICT and electronics retail sector: buyers currently have no unified digital touchpoint to compare product availability and pricing across multiple local stores, while sellers lack a low-friction, high-visibility digital sales channel.

Competitive benchmarking confirms the commercial viability of this model. Jumia Kenya has demonstrated that multi-vendor seller onboarding and bulk catalogue upload drives rapid inventory growth in African markets. PriceCheck South Africa has proven that real-time price monitoring across retailers, refreshed multiple times daily, is a primary driver of buyer retention. Google Shopping has established feed-based product ingestion as the industry standard for bulk sellers onboarding at scale. Price.com has demonstrated that AI-assisted buying tools comparing over one billion products significantly increase conversion rates. Shopify has confirmed that unlimited SKU management with integrated payment gateways removes the primary friction points in seller adoption.

This platform synthesizes all five proven patterns into a single, locally optimized solution.

Business Objective

Enable a seller to register, agree to commission terms, and have their product catalogue live and discoverable by buyers within 30 minutes of account approval — following the proven Jumia Vendor Hub and Shopify onboarding patterns.

Grow the seller base and increase platform product catalogue breadth at scale

Primary Actor

Seller / Vendor

Trigger

Seller visits platform and clicks 'Register as Seller'

Business Value

Drives product inventory depth, buyer engagement, and commission revenue

Success Outcome

Seller products are live and discoverable within 30 minutes of approval

Failure Outcome

Seller abandons due to complexity; catalogue remains offline; revenue lost

Sellers must accept commission policy. Products must carry exact names, pricing, and stock. 30-day price refresh required.

Jumia Vendor Center self-onboarding; Shopify unlimited SKU plan; Google Shopping feed upload

PriceCheck ZA real-time comparison; Price.com AI Buying Tool; Google Shopping lowest-price highlight

Jumia Admin Suite; Shopify Admin Dashboard; PriceCheck seller verification workflow

USE CASE — Register as Seller  [UC-01]

Use Case ID

UC-01

Use Case Name

Register as Seller

Actor(s)

Seller (Primary), Email System (Secondary)

Description

A new seller registers on the platform by providing business and personal details, selecting the Seller role, accepting the platform commission policy, and completing email verification.

User is not already registered. A User has valid email address and business information. Commission Policy has been published by the platform.

Main Flow

1. User navigates to Landing Page. 2. Clicks 'Register as Seller'. 3. Completes registration form: name, email, phone, business name, password. 4. Step 6: Email delivery fails → system prompts to resend. Step 7: Link expired → system offers renewal. Step 4: Seller declines commission terms → registration blocked; reason displayed.

HIGH

USE CASE — Upload Products via Excel (Bulk)  [UC-03]

Use Case ID

UC-03

Use Case Name

Upload Products via Excel (Bulk)

Actor(s)

Seller (Primary), File Processor (System)

Description

An authenticated seller uploads an Excel spreadsheet of product records. The system validates each row, processes valid entries, and generates a downloadable error report for rejected rows.

Preconditions

Seller is authenticated. Seller has downloaded and populated the platform's standard Excel template.

Main Flow

1. Seller opens Vendor Hub → 'Product Management' → 'Bulk Upload'. 2. Downloads standard .xlsx template. 3. Populates template with product data. 4. Uploads file. 5. System validates each row for mandatory fields, data types, and price format. 6. System displays preview: valid row count, invalid row count, first 5 errors. 7. Seller confirms upload. 8. Valid products saved and listed as active. 9. Error report generated and available for download.

Alternate Flow

File format not .xlsx → rejected with format error. Mandatory field missing → row flagged invalid; others processed. File exceeds 10MB → rejected with size error. Seller cancels at step 7 → no records saved.

Postconditions

Valid products are live and discoverable by buyers. Seller receives email confirmation of upload summary.

Priority

HIGH

Benchmark Reference

Jumia KE Vendor Bulk Upload; Google Shopping Product Feed



USE CASE — Use AI Buying Tool  [UC-07]

Use Case ID

UC-07

Use Case Name

Use AI Buying Tool

Actor(s)

Buyer / Guest (Primary), AI Recommendation Engine (System)

Description

A user searches for a product and activates the AI Buying Tool which analyses all listings for that product across all stores and recommends the best-value option based on price, stock status, seller rating, and delivery speed.

Preconditions

At least one seller has the product listed as active. AI Buying Tool feature is enabled.

Main Flow

1. User searches for a product. 2. Comparison results page loads. 3. User clicks 'Find Best Value'. 4. AI Engine evaluates all active listings: price, stock status, seller rating, estimated delivery. 5. System surfaces top recommendation with a highlighted badge and a brief explanation ('Best value: lowest price from a 4.8★ seller with same-day delivery'). 6. User clicks 'Add to Cart' on the recommendation. 7. Product added to cart.

Alternate Flow

Step 4: Only one listing exists → tool shows that listing and notes no alternatives found. Step 4: All listings out of stock → tool returns 'No in-stock listings found; set a price-drop alert?' prompt.

Postconditions

User has received an AI-driven product recommendation. A product may be in cart. Recommendation data is logged for model improvement.

Priority

HIGH

Benchmark Reference

Price.com AI Buying Tool (1B+ product comparison engine)



USE CASE — Checkout & Pay  [UC-09]

Use Case ID

UC-09

Use Case Name

Checkout & pay

Actor(s)

Buyer (Primary), Payment Gateway — Primary (Secondary), Payment Gateway — Fallback (Secondary), Sellers (Secondary)

Description

An authenticated buyer reviews their cart, confirms delivery address, selects payment method, and completes payment through the integrated primary gateway, with automatic failover to the secondary gateway if needed.

Preconditions

Buyer is authenticated. Cart contains at least one in-stock item.

Main Flow

1. Buyer navigates to Cart. 2. Review items, quantities, per-store subtotals, delivery estimate, and grand total. 3. Proceeds to Checkout. 4. Confirms or enters delivery address. 5. Selects payment method (card, mobile money). 6. Reviews final order summary. 7. Confirms payment. 8. Primary gateway processes transaction. 9. System records order with status 'Processing'. 10. Confirmation email sent to buyer; order notification sent to each relevant seller. 11. Inventory decremented per seller.

Alternate Flow

Step 8: Primary gateway fails → system automatically retries via secondary gateway; buyer notified of switch. Step 8: Both gateways fail → buyer notified; cart retained for retry. Stock depleted between cart-add and checkout → buyer notified; item flagged for removal.

Postconditions

Order created with status 'Processing'. Buyer receives confirmation. Sellers notified. Inventory updated. Commission recorded for platform.

Priority

HIGH

Benchmark Reference

Jumia KE Checkout; Shopify multi-gateway failover pattern

User tries to review without completed purchase → blocked with 'Verified purchase required'. Admin flags review → hidden pending investigation. Duplicate review attempt → system blocks with 'You have already reviewed this product'.

Postconditions

Discussion thread or review is live. Seller's aggregate rating is updated. Admin notified of any flagged content.

10. Assumptions, Constraints & Risks

Type

Item

Detail

Assumption

Seller Responsibility

Sellers are responsible for the accuracy of product names, prices, and stock levels as submitted.

Assumption

Template Adoption

All sellers will use the platform-provided Excel template without structural modification.

Assumption

Internet Access

All users have reliable internet access to use the platform.

Assumption

Price Honesty

Sellers will update prices promptly. Platform enforces 30-day confirmation cycle to mitigate stale data.

Constraint

Technology Stack

Development must use standard web technologies compatible with widely available cloud infrastructure.

Constraint

Budget — Phase 1 & 2

Initial delivery scope is limited to Phase 1 and Phase 2 features per agreed milestones.

Constraint

Currency

Platform defaults to US$; multi-currency support is a post-launch enhancement.

Risk

Low Seller Adoption

Sellers may resist digital onboarding. Mitigation: Assisted Upload Service for first catalogue (FR-02.10). [Lesson from Jumia KE onboarding]

Risk

Price Data Accuracy

Sellers may fail to update prices promptly. Mitigation: 30-day mandatory confirmation; automated admin alerts (FR-08.5).

Risk

Payment Gateway Downtime

Primary gateway outage affects checkout. Mitigation: Secondary fallback gateway integrated (FR-04.4). [Benchmark: Shopify multi-gateway]

Risk

AI Tool Trust

Buyers may not trust AI recommendations. Mitigation: Recommendation explanations displayed in plain language (UC-07 Main Flow). [Benchmark: Price.com]

Risk

Content Moderation Load

Social module generates user content requiring moderation at scale. Mitigation: Admin alert system and community flagging (FR-06.5, FR-08.2).
